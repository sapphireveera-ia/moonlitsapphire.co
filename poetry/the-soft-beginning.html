the soft beginning.

As the sun lowered its gaze,
amber light brushed the cheeks of two quiet souls.
Silence learned how to speak,
and a single look unraveled a thousand stories.

“May I touch your hair?”
A timid nod answered the pulse of a shy heart.
Sun-warmed strands, tinged with soft brown,
rested gently in the cradle of dusk.

Fingers lingered in reverent stillness,
the wind exhaled in whispers.
One laugh, one smile
and time, understanding, held its breath.

Footsteps home dissolved into violet twilight,
while the earth released its memory into the air.
A tender grip on a dangling strap,
warmer than a thousand unspoken words.

No vows were asked for, no reasons demanded
only a silence that recognized itself.

The evening thinned and drifted away,
yet the feeling refused to loosen its hold.
Even now, the ears remember
laughter beneath a blushing sky,
calling rose-colored warmth to gentle cheeks.

Was it merely an afternoon too soft to endure?
Or something quietly taking root,
growing without permission,
without a name.
