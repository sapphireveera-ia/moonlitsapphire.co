stillness.

Late Morning, almost noon
A tender breeze drifts unhurried,
tracing warmth across my face
and the quiet hours of the day.

The sky glows in a half-lit hush,
and the leaves sway, soft,
as though careful not to disturb the moment.

Two souls cross paths without intention,
a flicker of greeting,
a glance too brief to hold,
yet wrapped within those timid smiles,
something gentle chooses to remain.

No vows, no names,
only a breath of time suspended in the air.

And somehow,
the sky brightens beyond its nature,
as though it, too, sensed
how two wondering hearts
touched for a heartbeat,
and then parted with the light.
